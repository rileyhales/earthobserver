The goal of the Global Land Data Assimilation System (GLDAS) is to ingest satellite- and ground-based observational data products, using advanced land surface modeling and data assimilation techniques, in order to generate optimal fields of land surface states and fluxes (Rodell et al., 2004a). The software, which has been streamlined and parallelized by the Land Information System (LIS) sister project, drives multiple, offline (not coupled to the atmosphere) land surface models, integrates a huge quantity of observation based data, executes globally at high resolutions (2.5-degrees to 1 km), and is capable of producing results in near-real time. (from ldas.gsfc.nasa.gov)
"The Global Forecast System (GFS) is a weather forecast model produced by the National Centers for Environmental Prediction (NCEP). Dozens of atmospheric and land-soil variables are available through this dataset, from temperatures, winds, and precipitation to soil moisture and atmospheric ozone concentration. The entire globe is covered by the GFS at a base horizontal resolution of 18 miles (28 kilometers) between grid points, which is used by the operational forecasters who predict weather out to 16 days in the future. Horizontal resolution drops to 44 miles (70 kilometers) between grid point for forecasts between one week and two weeks." (from ncdc.noaa.gov)
